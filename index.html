Housing in America

The Housing Crisis

Causes More Information

Over 100 million people are homeless in the world. About 582,000 People in America with 30% coming from California. There is an increasing gap between housing costs and income. In the 1970s many communities had affordable housing. Since the mid 1980s rents have continued to rise while wages have had stagnant growth.

Causes

ADDICTION

The most common stereotype of chronically homeless people is that they are drug and alcohol addicts. 68% of U.S. cities report that addiction is a their single largest cause of homelessness. A formerly homeless addict is likely to return to homelessness unless they deal with the addiction. Treatment programs are needed that treat the root causes of addiction and help men and women find a way back home.

FORECLOSURE

People who have jobs are finding themselves upside down with their mortgages. From 2008 to 2009, foreclosures jumped by 32%. A 2009 survey estimates that as many as 10% of people seeking help from homeless organizations do so due to foreclosure.
